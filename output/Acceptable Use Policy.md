## SECTION 1 — PURPOSE OF THIS POLICY

This AUP defines acceptable behavior, prohibited activities, and safety requirements to ensure:

**(a)** platform security and integrity;

**(b)** lawful and responsible use of the Services;

**(c)** protection of Users, third parties, and the Company;

**(d)** compliance with Applicable Law, including data protection laws, export control laws, and intellectual property laws;

**(e)** prevention of abuse, fraud, and harmful conduct.

## SECTION 2 — PROHIBITED CONDUCT

Users shall NOT engage in any of the following activities:

### 2.1 Illegal Activities

**(a)** Violating any Applicable Law, regulation, court order, or legal obligation;

**(b)** Engaging in fraud, money laundering, embezzlement, or illegal financial schemes;

**(c)** Using the Services for illegal gambling, extremist activity, human trafficking, or unlawful trade in controlled substances, weapons, or other prohibited goods;

**(d)** Facilitating or promoting illegal activities.

### 2.2 Harmful or Abusive Behavior

**(a)** Harassment, threats, stalking, intimidation, or bullying of any person;

**(b)** Discrimination, hate speech, or incitement to violence based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic;

**(c)** Posting, transmitting, or distributing harmful, violent, abusive, or graphic content;

**(d)** Doxxing or revealing private information about individuals without their consent;

**(e)** Impersonating any person or entity, or falsely representing affiliation with any person or entity.

### 2.3 Security Violations

**(a)** Attempting to bypass, circumvent, or disable authentication mechanisms, access controls, or security features;

**(b)** Conducting unauthorized penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, or security probing without prior written permission from the Company (see Section 10 for responsible disclosure);

**(c)** Uploading, transmitting, or distributing malware, viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, ransomware, keyloggers, or any other malicious code;

**(d)** Conducting or facilitating distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks, spoofing, man-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks, or other network attacks;

**(e)** Attempting to gain unauthorized access to the Services, other Users' accounts, or the Company's systems or networks;

**(f)** Interfering with or disrupting the integrity, performance, or availability of the Services.

### 2.4 Platform Manipulation and Abuse

**(a)** Creating fake accounts, using bots, or employing automated tools to manipulate the Services or simulate human activity;

**(b)** Interfering with normal platform operations or degrading the experience of other Users;

**(c)** Exploiting vulnerabilities, bugs, or design flaws in the Services for any purpose;

**(d)** Attempting to reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or derive source code from the Services;

**(e)** Scraping, crawling, or harvesting data from the Services using automated means without authorization.

### 2.5 Intellectual Property Violations

**(a)** Infringing copyrights, trademarks, patents, trade secrets, or other Intellectual Property Rights of the Company or any third party;

**(b)** Uploading, sharing, or distributing content without possessing the necessary rights, licenses, or permissions;

**(c)** Removing, obscuring, or altering proprietary notices, labels, or watermarks.

### 2.6 High-Risk or Dangerous Uses

The Services shall NOT be used for:

**(a)** critical infrastructure operations (e.g., power grids, water treatment, transportation systems);

**(b)** life-support systems, medical devices, or emergency medical services;

**(c)** emergency response systems (e.g., 911, emergency dispatch);

**(d)** nuclear facilities or nuclear weapons development;

**(e)** weapons development, targeting, or control systems;

**(f)** surveillance, monitoring, or tracking of individuals without legal authority or consent;

**(g)** any application where failure or malfunction could result in death, personal injury, or significant property or environmental damage.

### 2.7 AI/ML Training and Data Misuse

**(a)** Using the Services or any data obtained from the Services (including Customer Data, User Data, or Personal Data) to train, develop, test, or improve artificial intelligence, machine learning, or algorithmic models, except as strictly necessary to use the Services for their intended purpose;

**(b)** This prohibition aligns with MSA Section 4.2(g) and DPA Section 5.2.

### 2.8 Biometric Data Processing

**(a)** Processing Biometric Data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person is strictly prohibited;

**(b)** This prohibition aligns with DPA Section 5.1.

### 2.9 Children's Data

**(a)** Knowingly collecting, processing, or storing Children's Personal Data (individuals under 13 in the United States or under 16 in the European Economic Area and United Kingdom) without implementing appropriate safeguards and obtaining required parental or guardian consent;

**(b)** This requirement aligns with DPA Section 5.3 and Applicable Data Protection Laws, including COPPA and GDPR Article 8.

### 2.10 Deepfakes and Synthetic Media

**(a)** Creating, uploading, sharing, or distributing deepfakes, synthetic media, or AI-generated content that impersonates real individuals without their explicit consent;

**(b)** Using the Services to create misleading, deceptive, or fraudulent synthetic media;

**(c)** Manipulating images, videos, or audio to falsely depict individuals saying or doing things they did not say or do.

### 2.11 Cryptocurrency and Blockchain Restrictions

**(a)** Using the Services for cryptocurrency mining, including but not limited to Bitcoin, Ethereum, or any other cryptocurrency or token mining;

**(b)** Using the Services for fraudulent cryptocurrency schemes, pump-and-dump schemes, rug pulls, Ponzi schemes, or pyramid schemes involving digital assets;

**(c)** Using the Services to facilitate illegal initial coin offerings (ICOs), token sales, or unregistered securities offerings;

**(d)** Legitimate use of cryptocurrency wallets or payment processing as part of the Services' intended functionality is permitted, provided such use complies with all Applicable Laws.

### 2.12 Discriminatory Automated Decision-Making

**(a)** Using the Services to engage in Automated Decision-Making that produces discriminatory, biased, or unlawful outcomes based on protected characteristics;

**(b)** This requirement aligns with DPA Section 6 and Applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 2.13 Export Control Violations

**(a)** Accessing or using the Services from, or exporting data to, any country or territory subject to comprehensive U.S. trade embargoes or sanctions, including Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and the Crimea region of Ukraine;

**(b)** Violating any export control laws or regulations, including the U.S. Export Administration Regulations (EAR) and International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR);

**(c)** This requirement aligns with MSA Section 25.

## SECTION 6 — RESOURCE ABUSE

Users shall NOT:

**(a)** Consume excessive computational resources (CPU, memory, bandwidth, storage) in a manner that degrades the Services for other Users;

**(b)** Run processes or scripts that continuously consume resources without legitimate business purpose;

**(c)** Store excessive amounts of data beyond the usage limits specified in the Order Form;

**(d)** Generate excessive API requests, network traffic, or database queries that impact system performance;

**(e)** Use the Services in a manner that circumvents usage limits or billing mechanisms.

**Resource Abuse Thresholds:**

- Sustained CPU usage exceeding 90% for more than 4 hours without justification;
- Storage usage exceeding 150% of allocated quota;
- API requests exceeding 200% of rate limits within a 24-hour period;
- Bandwidth usage exceeding 300% of allocated quota within a billing cycle.

### 8.1 Enforcement Actions

Upon discovering or receiving notice of a violation of this AUP, the Company may, at its sole discretion, take any of the following actions:

**(a)** Issue a written warning to the User;

**(b)** Remove, block, or disable access to violating content;

**(c)** Suspend the User's account or access to the Services;

**(d)** Terminate the User's account and the applicable agreement (MSA, EULA, or Terms of Service);

**(e)** Throttle, restrict, or limit the User's access to specific features or resources;

**(f)** Notify law enforcement, regulatory authorities, or other appropriate third parties;

**(g)** Pursue legal remedies, including claims for damages, injunctive relief, or specific performance;

**(h)** Cooperate with legal investigations or regulatory inquiries.

### 8.2 Notice and Cure Period

**(a)** For non-critical violations that do not pose an immediate security risk, the Company shall provide the User with written notice of the violation and an opportunity to cure within twenty-four (24) hours.

**(b)** For critical violations involving security risks, illegal activity, harm to others, or violations of Sections 2.3, 2.6, 2.7, 2.8, 2.9, 2.10, 2.11, 3(b), or 3(c), the Company may take immediate action without prior notice or opportunity to cure.

**(c)** Failure to cure a non-critical violation within the specified cure period may result in suspension or termination.

### 8.3 Appeal Process

**(a)** If the Company suspends or terminates a User's account for an alleged AUP violation, the User may submit a written appeal to the contact specified in Section 9 within ten (10) Business Days of receiving notice of the enforcement action.

**(b)** The appeal must include:

- **(i)** the User's account information;
- **(ii)** a detailed explanation of why the User believes the enforcement action was in error;
- **(iii)** any supporting evidence or documentation.

**(c)** The Company shall review the appeal and respond within fifteen (15) Business Days.

**(d)** The Company's decision on the appeal is final and binding.

**(e)** During the appeal process, the suspension or termination remains in effect unless the Company decides otherwise.

### 8.4 No Liability for Enforcement

The Company shall not be liable for any damages, losses, or consequences resulting from enforcement actions taken in good faith to address AUP violations.

## SECTION 10 — RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE OF SECURITY VULNERABILITIES

### 10.1 Safe Harbor

The Company encourages responsible disclosure of security vulnerabilities. Security researchers who comply with this policy will not face legal action for:

**(a)** conducting good-faith security research;

**(b)** reporting vulnerabilities in accordance with this Section 10.

### 10.2 Responsible Disclosure Guidelines

Security researchers must:

**(a)** Report vulnerabilities privately to the Company at [INSERT SECURITY EMAIL] before public disclosure;

**(b)** Provide the Company with a reasonable period (at least ninety (90) days) to address the vulnerability before public disclosure;

**(c)** Not exploit vulnerabilities beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the issue;

**(d)** Not access, modify, or delete data belonging to other Users;

**(e)** Not disrupt or degrade the Services;

**(f)** Not conduct testing on production systems without prior written permission;

**(g)** Comply with all Applicable Laws.

### 10.3 Out of Scope

The following activities are NOT covered by the safe harbor and are prohibited:

**(a)** Social engineering, phishing, or physical attacks;

**(b)** Denial-of-service (DoS/DDoS) attacks;

**(c)** Spamming or mass account creation;

**(d)** Testing on third-party services or systems;

**(e)** Violations of privacy or data protection laws.

### 10.4 Recognition

The Company may, at its discretion, recognize security researchers who report valid vulnerabilities by:

**(a)** publicly acknowledging the researcher (with permission);

**(b)** listing the researcher on a security acknowledgments page.

The Company does not currently offer monetary bug bounty rewards.
