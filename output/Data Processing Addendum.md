# DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM

**Effective Date:** [As specified in Schedule 1]

---

## SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

### 1.1 Definitions

**(a) "Applicable Data Protection Laws"**  
Means all applicable privacy and data protection laws, including:

- **(i)** EU GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679);
- **(ii)** UK GDPR and UK Data Protection Act 2018;
- **(iii)** UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR);
- **(iv)** EU ePrivacy Directive;
- **(v)** CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act);
- **(vi)** Applicable U.S. state consumer privacy laws with requirements materially similar to the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA);
- **(vii)** Nevada NRS 603A;
- **(viii)** Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA);
- **(ix)** Taiwan PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act);
- **(x)** any other laws specified in Schedule 1.

**(b) "Automated Decision-Making"**  
Means any decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects a Data Subject.

**(c) "Biometric Data"**  
Means Personal Data resulting from specific technical processing relating to the physical, physiological, or behavioral characteristics of a natural person, including facial images, fingerprints, voiceprints, iris scans, or retina scans.

**(d) "Children's Personal Data"**  
Means Personal Data relating to individuals under the age of thirteen (13) in the United States or under the age of sixteen (16) in the European Economic Area and United Kingdom.

**(e) "Client" or "Controller"**  
Means the business customer that determines the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data.

**(f) "Company" or "Processor"**  
Means the entity identified in Schedule 1 that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Client.

**(g) "Data Protection Officer (DPO)"**  
Means the individual designated by the Company to oversee data protection compliance, as specified in Schedule 1.

**(h) "Data Subject"**  
Means the individual to whom Personal Data relates.

**(i) "DPA"**  
Means this Data Processing Addendum together with Schedule 1 (Specific Provisions), incorporated into the MSA.

**(j) "EEA"**  
Means the European Economic Area.

**(k) "Instructions"**  
Means written, documented instructions issued by the Client to the Company regarding the Processing of Personal Data.

**(l) "International Transfer"**  
Means any transfer of Personal Data outside the jurisdiction where it originated.

**(m) "MSA"**  
Means the Master Service Agreement to which this DPA is attached.

**(n) "Personal Data"**  
Means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, as defined under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

**(o) "Processing"**  
Means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**(p) "Recovery Point Objective (RPO)"**  
Has the meaning given in the SLA.

**(q) "Recovery Time Objective (RTO)"**  
Has the meaning given in the SLA.

**(r) "Retention Period"**  
Means the period for which Personal Data is retained, as defined in Schedule 1 or the MSA.

**(s) "SCCs"**  
Means EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914).

**(t) "Security Incident"**  
Means any confirmed breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

**(u) "Security Measures"**  
Means the technical and organizational measures described in Section 12 and Schedule 1.

**(v) "Special Category Data"**  
Means Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health, or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation, as defined in GDPR Article 9.

**(w) "Subprocessor"**  
Means any third party engaged by the Company to Process Personal Data on behalf of the Client.

**(x) "UK Addendum"**  
Means the International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Commission Standard Contractual Clauses issued by the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

### 1.2 Interpretation

**(a)** Capitalized terms not defined in this DPA have the meanings set forth in the MSA.

**(b)** References to laws include amendments, replacements, and successor legislation.

**(c)** References to "Controller" and "Processor" follow GDPR terminology, without prejudice to local law variations (e.g., "Business" and "Service Provider" under CCPA/CPRA).

**(d)** In case of conflict between the DPA and the MSA with respect to the Processing of Personal Data, the DPA prevails.

**(e)** Schedule 1 controls where variables are configurable.

**(f)** In case of translation discrepancies, the English version prevails.

**(g)** References to "writing" or "written" include email.

## SECTION 2 — SCOPE AND ROLES

### 2.1 Application

This DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data by the Company on behalf of the Client in connection with the Services.

### 2.2 Roles

**(a)** The Client acts as the Controller (or "Business" under CCPA/CPRA) and determines the purposes and means of Processing.

**(b)** The Company acts as the Processor (or "Service Provider" under CCPA/CPRA) and Processes Personal Data only on behalf of and in accordance with the Client's documented Instructions.

### 2.3 Nature and Purpose of Processing

**(a)** The nature and purpose of Processing are to provide the Services as described in the MSA.

**(b)** The types of Personal Data and categories of Data Subjects are specified in Schedule 1.

---

## SECTION 3 — CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Lawful Basis

The Client shall ensure it has a lawful basis for Processing Personal Data under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 3.2 Accuracy and Minimization

The Client shall ensure Personal Data is accurate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes for which it is Processed.

### 3.3 Instructions

**(a)** The Client shall provide documented Instructions for Processing.

**(b)** The Company shall not be responsible for violations caused by unclear, incomplete, or unlawful Instructions.

**(c)** If the Company believes an Instruction violates Applicable Data Protection Laws, the Company shall promptly notify the Client.

### 3.4 Data Subject Notices

The Client is responsible for providing Data Subjects with all required notices regarding the Processing of their Personal Data.

---

## SECTION 4 — PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

### 4.1 Processing on Documented Instructions

The Company shall Process Personal Data only:

**(a)** on the Client's documented Instructions; or

**(b)** as required by Applicable Law (with prior written notice to the Client unless prohibited by law).

### 4.2 Confidentiality

The Company shall ensure that all personnel authorized to Process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations, whether by contract or statutory duty.

### 4.3 Security Measures (GDPR Art. 32)

The Company shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational Security Measures as described in Section 12.

### 4.4 Subprocessors

**(a)** The Company may engage Subprocessors listed in Schedule 1.

**(b)** The Company shall impose data protection obligations on Subprocessors that are equivalent to those in this DPA.

**(c)** The Company shall notify the Client of any new Subprocessor at least thirty (30) days in advance, in accordance with MSA Section 10.4(b).

**(d)** The Client may object to a new Subprocessor on reasonable grounds related to data protection or security.

**(e)** If the Client's objection is not resolved, the Client may terminate the affected Services without penalty.

### 4.5 International Transfers

The Company shall not transfer Personal Data outside its region of origin unless appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with Section 10.

### 4.6 Assistance with Client Obligations

The Company shall, taking into account the nature of Processing and the information available to the Company, assist the Client in fulfilling the Client's obligations regarding:

**(a)** Data Subject rights requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection);

**(b)** Security Incident notifications to supervisory authorities and Data Subjects;

**(c)** data protection impact assessments (DPIAs);

**(d)** prior consultations with supervisory authorities.

### 4.7 Data Subject Rights Requests

**(a)** If the Company receives a Data Subject rights request directly, the Company shall promptly notify the Client and shall not respond to the request without the Client's prior written authorization.

**(b)** The Company shall provide reasonable assistance to the Client in responding to Data Subject rights requests, including by providing access to relevant Personal Data within ten (10) Business Days of the Client's request.

### 4.8 Recordkeeping

The Company shall maintain records of Processing activities sufficient to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and Applicable Data Protection Laws.

---

## SECTION 5 — PROHIBITED PROCESSING

### 5.1 Biometric Data

**(a)** The Company shall not Process Biometric Data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person.

**(b)** If the Client uploads Biometric Data to the Services, the Client acknowledges that such Processing is prohibited and the Company may delete or refuse to Process such data.

### 5.2 AI/ML Training Prohibition

**(a)** The Company shall not use, disclose, or Process Personal Data to train, develop, or improve artificial intelligence, machine learning, or algorithmic models, except as strictly necessary to provide the Services to the Client.

**(b)** This prohibition aligns with MSA Section 4.2(g).

### 5.3 Children's Personal Data

**(a)** The Client shall not knowingly provide Children's Personal Data to the Company without implementing appropriate safeguards and obtaining required parental or guardian consent.

**(b)** Age thresholds:

- **(i)** United States: under thirteen (13) years of age (COPPA);
- **(ii)** European Economic Area and United Kingdom: under sixteen (16) years of age (GDPR Article 8).

**(c)** If the Company becomes aware that Children's Personal Data has been Processed without appropriate consent, the Company shall notify the Client and, upon the Client's instruction, delete such data.

### 5.4 Special Category Data

**(a)** The Company shall not Process Special Category Data (as defined in GDPR Article 9) unless:

- **(i)** explicitly authorized in Schedule 1; and
- **(ii)** the Client has obtained all required consents or established another lawful basis under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

**(b)** If Special Category Data is authorized, the Company shall implement enhanced Security Measures appropriate to the sensitivity of such data.

---

## SECTION 6 — AUTOMATED DECISION-MAKING

### 6.1 Restrictions

**(a)** The Company shall not engage in Automated Decision-Making that produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects Data Subjects unless:

- **(i)** explicitly authorized by the Client in writing; and
- **(ii)** appropriate safeguards are in place, including the right to human review.

**(b)** If Automated Decision-Making is authorized, the Company shall:

- **(i)** provide meaningful information about the logic involved;
- **(ii)** enable human intervention upon request;
- **(iii)** allow Data Subjects to express their point of view and contest the decision.

### 6.2 Profiling

The Company shall not engage in profiling activities that could result in discrimination or adverse effects on Data Subjects without the Client's prior written authorization and appropriate safeguards.

---

## SECTION 7 — SECURITY INCIDENT MANAGEMENT

### 7.1 Notification Timeline

**(a)** The Company shall notify the Client of any Security Incident within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the Security Incident, in accordance with MSA Section 14.1 and SLA Section 10.1.

**(b)** Notification shall be sent to the security contact specified in Schedule 1.

### 7.2 Notification Contents

Notification shall include, to the extent known:

**(a)** the nature and scope of the Security Incident;

**(b)** the categories and approximate number of affected Data Subjects and Personal Data records;

**(c)** the likely consequences of the Security Incident;

**(d)** the measures taken or proposed to address the Security Incident and mitigate its effects;

**(e)** contact details for further information.

### 7.3 Cooperation and Assistance

**(a)** The Company shall take reasonable steps to investigate, mitigate, and remediate the Security Incident.

**(b)** The Company shall cooperate with the Client in fulfilling any obligations to notify supervisory authorities and Data Subjects under GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, Taiwan PDPA, and other Applicable Data Protection Laws.

**(c)** The Company shall provide periodic updates to the Client regarding the status of the investigation and remediation.

### 7.4 Documentation

The Company shall maintain detailed records of all Security Incidents, including facts, effects, and remedial actions taken.

---

## SECTION 8 — AUDIT RIGHTS

### 8.1 Audit Frequency and Notice

**(a)** The Client may audit the Company's compliance with this DPA once annually.

**(b)** The Client shall provide at least thirty (30) days' advance written notice.

**(c)** Audits shall be conducted during normal business hours and in a manner that minimizes disruption to the Company's operations.

### 8.2 Third-Party Auditors

**(a)** Audits may be conducted by an independent third-party auditor engaged by the Client.

**(b)** The auditor shall execute a confidentiality agreement with the Company.

**(c)** The Company may object to an auditor that is a direct competitor.

### 8.3 Audit Scope

Audits may include:

**(a)** review of Security Measures;

**(b)** review of Subprocessor agreements;

**(c)** review of Processing records;

**(d)** inspection of relevant facilities (subject to security and confidentiality restrictions).

### 8.4 Audit Costs

**(a)** The Client shall bear the costs of audits, except as provided in Section 8.4(b).

**(b)** If an audit reveals material non-compliance with this DPA, the Company shall reimburse the Client's reasonable audit costs.

### 8.5 Alternative to Audit

In lieu of an on-site audit, the Company may provide the Client with:

**(a)** current SOC 2 Type II report;

**(b)** ISO 27001 certification;

**(c)** completed third-party security questionnaire;

**(d)** other evidence of compliance reasonably acceptable to the Client.

---

## SECTION 9 — DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

### 9.1 Access

The Company shall assist the Client in responding to Data Subject access requests by providing relevant Personal Data in a commonly used electronic format within ten (10) Business Days of the Client's request.

### 9.2 Rectification

The Company shall, upon the Client's instruction, rectify inaccurate Personal Data without undue delay.

### 9.3 Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

The Company shall, upon the Client's instruction, delete Personal Data without undue delay, except where retention is required by Applicable Law.

### 9.4 Restriction of Processing

The Company shall, upon the Client's instruction, restrict Processing of Personal Data where required by Applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 9.5 Data Portability

**(a)** The Company shall assist the Client in responding to Data Subject data portability requests.

**(b)** The Company shall provide Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, including:

- **(i)** JSON;
- **(ii)** CSV;
- **(iii)** XML.

**(c)** Data portability requests shall be fulfilled within thirty (30) days of the Client's instruction.

### 9.6 Objection

The Company shall, upon the Client's instruction, cease Processing Personal Data where a Data Subject has exercised the right to object under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 9.7 Fees

The Company may charge reasonable fees for assistance with Data Subject rights requests that are manifestly unfounded, excessive, or repetitive, as permitted by Applicable Data Protection Laws.

## SECTION 10 — INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

### 10.1 General Principle

The Company shall not transfer Personal Data outside its region of origin unless such transfer complies with Applicable Data Protection Laws and appropriate safeguards are in place.

### 10.2 Data Residency

**(a)** Personal Data shall be stored and Processed in the data region(s) specified in MSA Schedule 1, Section 7.2.

**(b)** The Company shall not transfer Personal Data outside the designated region(s) without the Client's prior written consent, except as necessary to provide the Services and in accordance with this Section 10.

### 10.3 Transfers from the EEA

**(a)** For transfers of Personal Data from the EEA to a third country not subject to an adequacy decision by the European Commission, the parties hereby incorporate by reference the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) set forth in Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, Module Two (Controller to Processor).

**(b)** For purposes of the SCCs:

- **(i)** The Client is the "data exporter";
- **(ii)** The Company is the "data importer";
- **(iii)** The optional clauses and docking clause are not selected;
- **(iv)** The governing law is as specified in MSA Schedule 1;
- **(v)** Disputes shall be resolved in the courts specified in MSA Schedule 1;
- **(vi)** Annex I, II, and III details are set forth in Schedule 1 of this DPA.

### 10.4 Transfers from the UK

**(a)** For transfers of Personal Data from the UK, the parties incorporate the UK ICO International Data Transfer Addendum ("UK Addendum") to the SCCs.

**(b)** The UK Addendum shall apply in addition to the SCCs for any transfers subject to UK GDPR.

### 10.5 Transfers from Switzerland

**(a)** For transfers of Personal Data from Switzerland, the parties agree that references to "EU," "Union," and "Member State" in the SCCs shall be interpreted to include Switzerland.

**(b)** The Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) shall have jurisdiction over transfers from Switzerland.

### 10.6 Additional Safeguards

The Company shall implement supplementary technical and organizational measures to ensure an adequate level of protection for international transfers, including:

**(a)** encryption of Personal Data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher;

**(b)** encryption of Personal Data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent;

**(c)** access controls limiting access to Personal Data on a need-to-know basis;

**(d)** minimization of data sets transferred;

**(e)** secure key management practices;

**(f)** regular security assessments of transfer mechanisms.

### 10.7 Client Authorization

By entering into this DPA, the Client authorizes international transfers of Personal Data to the extent necessary to provide the Services, subject to the safeguards set forth in this Section 10.

## SECTION 11 — SUBPROCESSORS

### 11.1 Approved Subprocessors

The Client authorizes the use of Subprocessors listed in Schedule 1.

### 11.2 New Subprocessors

**(a)** The Company shall notify the Client of any new Subprocessor at least thirty (30) days in advance, in accordance with MSA Section 10.4(b).

**(b)** Notification shall include:

- **(i)** the name and location of the Subprocessor;
- **(ii)** the Processing activities to be performed;
- **(iii)** the data protection safeguards in place.

**(c)** The Client may object to a new Subprocessor on reasonable grounds related to data protection or security by providing written notice within fifteen (15) days of receiving notification.

**(d)** If the parties cannot resolve the Client's objection within fifteen (15) days, the Client may:

- **(i)** terminate the affected Services without penalty; or
- **(ii)** request that the Company not use the objected Subprocessor for Processing the Client's Personal Data.

### 11.3 Subprocessor Contracts

**(a)** The Company shall impose data protection obligations on Subprocessors that are substantially equivalent to those in this DPA, including obligations regarding:

- **(i)** Processing only on documented instructions;
- **(ii)** confidentiality;
- **(iii)** Security Measures;
- **(iv)** Security Incident notification;
- **(v)** assistance with Data Subject rights;
- **(vi)** deletion or return of Personal Data.

**(b)** The Company shall remain fully liable to the Client for the performance of any Subprocessor.

### 11.4 Subprocessor List Updates

The Company shall maintain a current list of Subprocessors in Schedule 1 and shall update Schedule 1 within thirty (30) days of engaging a new Subprocessor.

## SECTION 12 — SECURITY MEASURES

### 12.1 Technical and Organizational Measures

The Company shall implement and maintain Security Measures appropriate to the risk, including:

**(a) Encryption**

- **(i)** TLS 1.2 or higher for encryption in transit;
- **(ii)** AES-256 or equivalent for encryption at rest;
- **(iii)** encrypted backups.

**(b) Access Controls**

- **(i)** Role-based access control (RBAC);
- **(ii)** Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for privileged accounts;
- **(iii)** Least-privilege access principle;
- **(iv)** Regular access reviews and revocation of unnecessary access.

**(c) Infrastructure Security**

- **(i)** Firewalls and network segmentation;
- **(ii)** DDoS protection;
- **(iii)** Intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS);
- **(iv)** Continuous vulnerability scanning;
- **(v)** Regular penetration testing (at least annually).

**(d) Monitoring and Logging**

- **(i)** Centralized logging of access to Personal Data;
- **(ii)** Security information and event management (SIEM);
- **(iii)** Audit trails retained for at least twelve (12) months;
- **(iv)** Real-time alerting for suspicious activities.

**(e) Backup and Business Continuity**

- **(i)** Daily encrypted backups of Personal Data;
- **(ii)** Geographically redundant backup storage;
- **(iii)** Disaster recovery procedures aligned with ISO 22301;
- **(iv)** Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of four (4) hours, as specified in SLA Section 5.3;
- **(v)** Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of twenty-four (24) hours, as specified in SLA Section 5.2.

**(f) Personnel Security**

- **(i)** Background checks on personnel with access to Personal Data, where permitted by Applicable Law;
- **(ii)** Mandatory data protection training for all personnel;
- **(iii)** Confidentiality agreements for all personnel.

**(g) Physical Security**

- **(i)** Secure data centers with restricted physical access;
- **(ii)** Video surveillance;
- **(iii)** Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control).

**(h) Secure Development**

- **(i)** Secure software development lifecycle (SDLC) aligned with OWASP best practices;
- **(ii)** Code reviews and security testing;
- **(iii)** Vulnerability management and patch management processes.

**(i) Pseudonymization and Minimization**

- **(i)** Pseudonymization of Personal Data where appropriate;
- **(ii)** Data minimization to Process only necessary Personal Data.

### 12.2 Security Certifications

**(a)** The Company shall maintain current ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certifications.

**(b)** Upon request, the Company shall provide the Client with copies of current certification reports, subject to confidentiality restrictions.

### 12.3 Annual Review

Security Measures shall be reviewed and updated at least annually to address evolving threats and technological developments.

### 12.4 Security Testing

The Company shall conduct:

**(a)** vulnerability assessments at least quarterly;

**(b)** penetration testing at least annually;

**(c)** remediation of critical and high-severity vulnerabilities within thirty (30) days of discovery.

## SECTION 13 — DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

### 13.1 Retention Period

The Company shall retain Personal Data only for:

**(a)** the duration of the MSA;

**(b)** any additional period required by Applicable Law;

**(c)** the Retention Period specified in Schedule 1.

### 13.2 Deletion Timeline

**(a)** Upon termination or expiration of the MSA, the Company shall delete all Personal Data within thirty (30) days, in accordance with MSA Section 21.4.

**(b)** The Client may request an extension of up to ninety (90) days for transition purposes, consistent with MSA Section 21.1.

### 13.3 Deletion Method

**(a)** Deletion shall be performed using secure deletion methods that render Personal Data unrecoverable.

**(b)** Backup copies containing Personal Data shall be overwritten or securely destroyed at the next scheduled backup cycle, but in no event later than ninety (90) days after the deletion date.

### 13.4 Certification of Deletion

Upon the Client's written request, the Company shall provide written certification that all Personal Data has been deleted in accordance with this Section 13.

### 13.5 Legal Hold Exception

**(a)** The Company may retain Personal Data to the extent required by Applicable Law, provided the Company:

- **(i)** notifies the Client of the legal requirement;
- **(ii)** limits retention to the minimum period required;
- **(iii)** continues to protect the Personal Data in accordance with this DPA.

**(b)** Upon expiration of the legal retention requirement, the Company shall delete the Personal Data in accordance with Section 13.2.

## SECTION 14 — BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY

### 14.1 Business Continuity Plan

The Company shall maintain a business continuity plan and disaster recovery plan aligned with ISO 22301 principles.

### 14.2 RTO and RPO

**(a)** The Company shall restore Services in accordance with the Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of four (4) hours and Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of twenty-four (24) hours, as specified in SLA Section 5.

**(b)** RTO and RPO commitments apply to non-Excluded Events as defined in the SLA.

### 14.3 Testing

The Company shall test disaster recovery procedures at least annually and shall provide test results to the Client upon request.

## SECTION 25 — SURVIVAL
