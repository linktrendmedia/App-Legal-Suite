## SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

### 1.1 Definitions

**(a) "Advertising Cookies"**  
Means Cookies used for cross-context behavioral advertising. These are disabled by default in logged-in areas unless explicitly enabled.

**(b) "Analytics Cookies"**  
Means Cookies used to generate aggregated, non-sensitive usage metrics.

**(c) "Applicable Cookie Laws"**  
Means all laws governing Cookies and tracking technologies, including:

- **(i)** EU ePrivacy Directive;
- **(ii)** EU GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679);
- **(iii)** UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR);
- **(iv)** UK GDPR and UK Data Protection Act 2018;
- **(v)** California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA);
- **(vi)** Applicable U.S. state consumer privacy laws with requirements materially similar to the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA);
- **(vii)** Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 603A (NRS 603A);
- **(viii)** Taiwan Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) tracking transparency requirements;
- **(ix)** any other applicable laws specified in Schedule 1.

**(d) "Biometric Data"**  
Has the meaning given in the Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and Registered User Privacy Policy.

**(e) "Children's Personal Data"**  
Has the meaning given in the Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and Registered User Privacy Policy, and means Personal Data relating to individuals who are:

- **(i)** under thirteen (13) years of age in the United States; or
- **(ii)** under sixteen (16) years of age in the European Economic Area and United Kingdom; or
- **(iii)** below the applicable age of digital consent in their jurisdiction.

**(f) "Company" or "We" or "Us"**  
Means the entity identified in Schedule 1 as the operator of the Services.

**(g) "Consent Banner"**  
Means the mechanism through which Users provide or withdraw consent for non-essential Cookies.

**(h) "Cookie Policy" or "Policy"**  
Means this Registered User Cookie Policy together with Schedule 1 (Specific Provisions).

**(i) "Cookies"**  
Means small text files, SDK identifiers, server-side session tokens, tracking pixels, local storage objects, and other technologies used to store or access information on a User's device.

**(j) "Device Data"**  
Means information collected automatically from the User's device when Cookies or similar technologies operate.

**(k) "First-Party Cookies"**  
Means Cookies set directly by the Company.

**(l) "Logged-In Areas"**  
Means all sections of the Services that require authentication, including dashboards, account pages, and user-restricted features.

**(m) "Personalization Cookies"**  
Means Cookies used to customize the User experience and feature behaviors.

**(n) "Persistent Cookies"**  
Means Cookies that remain stored on a User's device for the period specified in Schedule 1.

**(o) "Services"**  
Means the authenticated web and mobile applications operated by the Company.

**(p) "Session Cookies"**  
Means Cookies that expire when the User logs out or closes their browser.

**(q) "Strictly Necessary Cookies"**  
Means Cookies required for the operation of logged-in Services, exempt from consent under ePrivacy and PECR.

**(r) "Third-Party Cookies"**  
Means Cookies set by external providers integrated into the Services (e.g., analytics, payments, support widgets).

**(s) "Tracking Technologies"**  
Means SDKs, beacons, pixels, fingerprinting scripts, and any mechanism that stores or accesses device identifiers.

**(t) "User" or "You"**  
Means any Registered User who has created an account or uses authenticated features of the Services.

### 1.2 Interpretation

**(a)** Capitalized terms have the meanings set forth in this Policy or the Registered User Privacy Policy.

## SECTION 2 — COOKIE TYPES AND PURPOSES

### 2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies (No Consent Required)

These Cookies are essential for the operation of logged-in Services and do not require consent under Applicable Cookie Laws.

**(a) Authentication Cookies**  
- Maintain login sessions;
- Validate active sessions;
- Prevent unauthorized access.

**(b) Security & Fraud Prevention Cookies**  
- Detect suspicious behavior;
- Rate-limit repeated login attempts;
- Prevent CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery) and session hijacking.

**(c) Load Balancing Cookies**  
- Route traffic consistently;
- Maintain service stability.

**(d) Infrastructure Cookies**  
- Identify server region;
- Enable content delivery optimization.

### 2.2 Functional Cookies

These Cookies support core application functions.

**(a) Preference Cookies**  
- User language;
- Time zone;
- UI layout preferences.

**(b) Feature Enablement Cookies**  
- Store toggle states;
- Remember workflow progress;
- Enable beta features (optional).

### 2.3 Analytics Cookies (Configurable)

Used to understand performance and improve features. Consent may be required depending on jurisdiction and implementation.

**(a) First-Party Analytics**  
- In-house analytics tracking;
- Aggregated performance metrics;
- Non-sensitive user behavior insights.

**(b) Third-Party Analytics**  
May include:

- PostHog;
- Amplitude;
- Mixpanel;
- [INSERT OTHER ANALYTICS TOOLS IF APPLICABLE].

**Collected Data Types:**
- Page views;
- Interaction heatmaps;
- Conversion funnels;
- Error events (non-sensitive).

### 2.4 Personalization Cookies (Optional)

Used to customize the User experience. Consent may be required depending on jurisdiction.

**(a)** UI customization;
**(b)** Recall of recently used features;
**(c)** Adaptive dashboards;
**(d)** Recommendation modules (non-sensitive).

### 2.5 Advertising & Cross-Context Behavioral Cookies (OFF by Default)

Advertising technologies are **disabled by default** in logged-in areas unless explicitly enabled by the developer.

If enabled, the following apply:

**(a)** Retargeting pixels;
**(b)** Attribution tags;
**(c)** Cross-site identifiers.

**Must comply with:**
- CPRA "Share" opt-out;
- GDPR consent requirement;
- PECR prior-consent requirement.

### 2.6 Mobile SDK Equivalents

Mobile applications use SDK technologies instead of browser-based Cookies.

**Common SDKs include:**

**(a)** Analytics SDKs (PostHog, Amplitude);
**(b)** Crash reporting SDKs;
**(c)** Authentication tokens stored in secure local storage;
**(d)** Device identifiers (IDFA/GAID — disabled unless explicit consent).

**SDK Purposes mirror Cookie purposes:**
- Session management;
- Analytics;
- Personalization;
- Feature operation.

## SECTION 7 — CONSENT BANNER, PREFERENCES, AND WITHDRAWAL

### 7.1 Consent Banner

**(a)** The Company shall provide a Cookie Consent Banner in logged-in areas where required by Applicable Cookie Laws.

**(b)** The banner shall:

- **(i)** appear immediately upon login or first access;
- **(ii)** present Accept/Reject/Customize options;
- **(iii)** be granular by category;
- **(iv)** not preselect non-essential cookies;
- **(v)** operate consistently across web and mobile.

### 7.2 Consent Logging

**(a)** The Company shall retain consent logs documenting:

- **(i)** timestamp;
- **(ii)** user ID or pseudonymous identifier;
- **(iii)** consent selections;
- **(iv)** version of this Cookie Policy.

**(b)** Consent logs are retained for the period specified in Schedule 1, and then deleted in accordance with the thirty (30) day deletion timeline that applies across the suite.

### 7.3 Withdrawal of Consent

**(a)** Users may withdraw consent at any time via:

- **(i)** the Cookie Preference Center;
- **(ii)** Account settings;
- **(iii)** support request.

**(b)** Upon withdrawal:

- **(i)** non-essential cookies must be disabled;
- **(ii)** identifiers must be deleted or anonymized;
- **(iii)** tracking must cease immediately.

### 7.4 Revocation Behavior

Upon revocation:

**(a)** personalization resets;
**(b)** analytics tracking disabled;
**(c)** advertising identifiers cleared;
**(d)** persistent cookies deleted where technically possible.

### 7.5 Mobile App Opt-Out

**(a)** Mobile apps must provide a consent mechanism equivalent to the web.

**(b)** Where IDFA/GAID is used, the OS-level prompt must be respected:

- **(i)** App Tracking Transparency (ATT) on iOS;
- **(ii)** Advertising ID settings on Android.

### 8.3 Wyoming

The Company shall comply with Wyoming privacy requirements regarding tracking transparency and opt-out rights.

### 8.4 Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Montana, Oregon, Texas, Delaware

The Company shall comply with the respective state privacy laws, including:

**(a)** opt-out of targeted advertising;
**(b)** opt-out of sale of Personal Data;
**(c)** opt-out of profiling.
