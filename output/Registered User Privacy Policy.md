## SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

### 1.1 Definitions

**(a) "Applicable Data Protection Laws"**  
Means all applicable data protection and privacy laws, including but not limited to:

- **(i)** EU GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679);
- **(ii)** UK GDPR and UK Data Protection Act 2018;
- **(iii)** UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR);
- **(iv)** EU ePrivacy Directive;
- **(v)** California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA);
- **(vi)** Applicable U.S. state consumer privacy laws with requirements materially similar to the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA);
- **(vii)** Nevada Revised Statutes Chapter 603A (NRS 603A);
- **(viii)** Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA);
- **(ix)** Taiwan Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA);
- **(x)** any other applicable privacy laws specified in Schedule 1.

**(b) "Account"**  
Means the Registered User's profile, credentials, settings, and associated data.

**(c) "Automated Decision-Making"**  
Has the meaning given in the Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and includes any decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects or similarly significantly affects the Data Subject.

**(d) "Biometric Data"**  
Has the meaning given in the Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and means data generated by technical processing relating to the physical, physiological, or behavioral characteristics of a natural person, which allow or confirm the unique identification of that natural person, such as facial images, fingerprints, voiceprints, or iris scans.

**(e) "Children's Personal Data"**  
Has the meaning given in the Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and means Personal Data relating to individuals who are:

- **(i)** under thirteen (13) years of age in the United States (subject to COPPA); or
- **(ii)** under sixteen (16) years of age in the European Economic Area and United Kingdom (subject to GDPR/UK GDPR); or
- **(iii)** below the applicable age of digital consent in their jurisdiction.

**(f) "Company" or "We" or "Us"**  
Means the entity identified in Schedule 1 as the controller of Personal Data for Registered Users.

**(g) "Controller" or "Data Controller"**  
Means the entity determining the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data (typically the Company).

**(h) "Cookies"**  
Means tracking technologies used within authenticated areas, governed by the Registered User Cookie Policy.

**(i) "Data Protection Officer" or "DPO"**  
Means the individual designated by the Company to oversee data protection compliance and serve as the point of contact for Data Subjects and supervisory authorities.

**(j) "Data Subject"**  
Means the individual to whom Personal Data relates.

**(k) "Device Data"**  
Means technical data automatically collected when Users access the Services.

**(l) "Personal Data"**  
Means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"). An identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier, or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that natural person.

**(m) "Processor" or "Data Processor"**  
Means any third party that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Company.

**(n) "Processing"**  
Means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

**(o) "Privacy Policy" or "Policy"**  
Means this Registered User Privacy Policy together with Schedule 1 (Specific Provisions).

**(p) "Registered User" or "User" or "You"**  
Means any individual who has created an account, logged in, or uses authenticated features of the Services.

**(q) "Retention Period"**  
Means the period stated in Schedule 1 for which Personal Data is retained.

**(r) "Security Measures"**  
Means organizational and technical safeguards described in Schedule 1.

**(s) "Services"**  
Means the web and mobile applications operated by the Company, as specified in Schedule 1.

**(t) "Third-Party Services"**  
Means external tools, platforms, processors, or SDKs integrated into the Services.

**(u) "Usage Data"**  
Means behavioral and analytical data related to User interactions with the Services.

## SECTION 14 — USER RIGHTS

### 14.1 Global Rights

Users have the following rights where applicable under Applicable Data Protection Laws:

**(a) Right to Access**  
The right to obtain confirmation as to whether Personal Data is being processed and, if so, to access the Personal Data.

**(b) Right to Rectification**  
The right to obtain rectification of inaccurate Personal Data and to have incomplete Personal Data completed.

**(c) Right to Deletion**  
The right to obtain erasure of Personal Data under certain circumstances (see Section 11.2).

**(d) Right to Restriction of Processing**  
The right to obtain restriction of processing under certain circumstances.

**(e) Right to Object**  
The right to object to processing based on legitimate interests, direct marketing, or processing for scientific or historical research purposes.

**(f) Right to Data Portability**  
The right to receive Personal Data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit that data to another controller (see Section 14.8).

**(g) Right to Withdraw Consent**  
The right to withdraw consent at any time (without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal).

**(h) Right to Lodge a Complaint**  
The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (see Section 16).

### 14.2 GDPR-Specific Rights (EU/EEA)

Users in the EU/EEA have all rights referenced in Articles 12–23 of the GDPR.

### 14.3 UK GDPR Rights

Users in the UK have equivalent rights under the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018.

**(a)** Know what Personal Information is collected;

**(d)** Correct inaccurate information;
**(e)** Opt out of "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising;
**(f)** Request information about categories sold or disclosed;
**(g)** Limit use of Sensitive Personal Information;
**(h)** Non-discrimination for exercising CCPA/CPRA rights.

### 14.5 Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Montana, Oregon, Texas, Delaware

Residents of Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Montana, Oregon, Texas, and Delaware have similar rights under their respective state privacy laws, including:

**(a)** Right to access Personal Data;
