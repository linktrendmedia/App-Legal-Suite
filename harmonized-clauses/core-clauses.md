## PURPOSE

This document contains harmonized clause templates that must be used consistently across multiple agreements in the App Legal Suite. These clauses ensure structural consistency, reduce conflicts, and maintain professional quality.

## 1. FORCE MAJEURE

### Standard Template (B2B)

**32.1 Suspension of Performance**

Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement (other than payment obligations) to the extent such failure or delay is caused by a Force Majeure Event.

**32.2 Notice**

The party affected by a Force Majeure Event shall:

**(a)** promptly notify the other party in writing of the Force Majeure Event and its expected duration;

**(b)** use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance as soon as practicable.

**32.3 Termination**

If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than sixty (60) consecutive days, either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other party.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA

## 2. CONFIDENTIALITY

### Standard Template (B2B)

**11.1 Definition**

"Confidential Information" includes all non-public, proprietary, business, technical, financial, security, and operational information disclosed by either party, whether oral, written, electronic, or visual, including:

**(a)** the terms and conditions of this Agreement;

**(b)** Client Data;

**(c)** technical specifications, designs, and architectures;

**(d)** business plans, strategies, and financial information;

**(e)** customer lists and pricing information.

**11.2 Obligations**

Each party shall:

**(a)** protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

**(b)** not disclose Confidential Information except to employees, contractors, or advisors with a legitimate need to know and who are bound by confidentiality obligations;

**(c)** use Confidential Information solely for the performance of this Agreement.

**11.3 Exceptions**

Confidential Information does not include information that:

**(a)** is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;

**(b)** was lawfully known to the receiving party prior to disclosure;

**(c)** is independently developed by the receiving party without reference to the Confidential Information;

**(d)** is rightfully received from a third party without breach of confidentiality obligations;

**(e)** is required to be disclosed by Applicable Law, court order, or regulatory authority (provided the receiving party provides advance notice to the disclosing party where legally permissible).

**11.4 Duration**

The obligations in this Section shall survive for five (5) years following termination or expiration of this Agreement.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, DPA, EULA

## 3. DATA PROTECTION COMPLIANCE

### Standard Template (All Agreements)

**12.1 Compliance**

The parties shall comply with all Applicable Data Protection Laws, including:

**(a)** GDPR (EU General Data Protection Regulation);

**(b)** UK GDPR;

**(c)** CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act);

**(d)** VCDPA (Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act);

**(g)** UCPA (Utah Consumer Privacy Act);

**(h)** Nevada NRS 603A;

**(i)** other US state privacy laws;

**(j)** Taiwan PDPA;

**(k)** any additional laws listed in Schedule 1.

**12.2 Data Processing**

**(a)** Data Processing shall be governed by the Data Processing Addendum (DPA), which is incorporated into this Agreement by reference.

**(b)** In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the DPA with respect to the Processing of Client Data, the DPA shall prevail.

**12.3 Data Ownership**

The Client retains all ownership rights in Client Data. The Company acquires no rights in Client Data except as necessary to provide the Services.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, DPA, B2C ToS, Privacy Policies

## 4. SECURITY OBLIGATIONS

### Standard Template (B2B)

**13.1 Security Measures**

The Company shall implement and maintain industry-standard administrative, physical, and technical safeguards designed to protect Client Data, including:

**(a)** alignment with ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II standards;

**(b)** adherence to OWASP best practices for application security;

**(c)** encryption of Client Data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher;

**(d)** encryption of Client Data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent;

**(e)** regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing;

**(f)** secure software development lifecycle practices.

**13.2 Access Controls**

The Company shall:

**(a)** enforce role-based access controls (RBAC);

**(b)** require multi-factor authentication for privileged accounts;

**(c)** limit personnel access to Client Data on a need-to-know basis;

**(d)** conduct background checks on personnel with access to Client Data, where permitted by Applicable Law.

**13.3 Security Certifications**

**(a)** The Company shall maintain current ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certifications.

**(b)** Upon request, the Company shall provide the Client with copies of current certification reports, subject to confidentiality restrictions.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, DPA

## 5. SECURITY INCIDENT NOTIFICATION

### Standard Template (All Agreements)

**14.1 Notification**

**(a)** The Company shall notify the Client of any Security Incident within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the Security Incident.

**(b)** Notification shall include, to the extent known:

- **(i)** the nature and scope of the Security Incident;
- **(ii)** the categories and approximate number of affected data subjects and data records;
- **(iii)** the likely consequences of the Security Incident;
- **(iv)** the measures taken or proposed to address the Security Incident and mitigate its effects.

**14.2 Cooperation**

**(a)** The Company shall take reasonable steps to investigate, mitigate, and remediate the Security Incident.

**(b)** The Company shall cooperate with the Client in any required notifications to data subjects, regulators, or other third parties.

**(c)** The Company shall provide periodic updates to the Client regarding the status of the investigation and remediation.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, DPA, Privacy Policies

## 6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### Standard Template (B2B)

**18.1 Exclusion of Consequential Damages**

TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR ANY LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, WHETHER ARISING IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE), OR OTHERWISE, EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

**18.2 Liability Cap**

**(a)** EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SECTION 18.3, EACH PARTY'S TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY THE CLIENT TO THE COMPANY IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO LIABILITY.

**(b)** The liability cap in Schedule 1 may specify an alternative cap amount or calculation method, which shall supersede Section 18.2(a).

**18.3 Exceptions to Liability Cap**

The limitations in Sections 18.1 and 18.2 shall not apply to:

**(a)** either party's indemnification obligations under Section 17;

**(b)** either party's breach of Section 11 (Confidentiality);

**(c)** the Client's payment obligations under Section 5;

**(d)** either party's gross negligence or willful misconduct;

**(e)** fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;

**(f)** death or personal injury caused by negligence;

**(g)** liabilities that cannot be limited or excluded under Applicable Law.

**18.4 Mandatory Consumer Rights**

Nothing in this Section limits or excludes any statutory rights or remedies that cannot be waived or limited under Applicable Law.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, EULA, B2C ToS

## 7. INDEMNIFICATION

### Standard Template (B2B)

**17.1 Company Indemnity**

**(a)** The Company shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Client from and against any third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to:

- **(i)** any claim that the Services infringe or misappropriate any third-party Intellectual Property Rights; or
- **(ii)** the Company's breach of Section [X] (compliance with Applicable Laws).

**(b)** The Company's indemnification obligations under Section 17.1(a)(i) shall not apply to the extent the claim arises from:

- **(i)** modifications to the Services made by the Client or at the Client's direction;
- **(ii)** use of the Services in combination with third-party products, services, or data not provided or approved by the Company;
- **(iii)** the Client's breach of this Agreement; or
- **(iv)** Client Data.

**17.2 Client Indemnity**

The Client shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Company from and against any third-party claims, losses, damages, liabilities, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to:

**(a)** Client Data, including any claim that Client Data infringes or misappropriates third-party rights;

**(b)** the Client's breach of this Agreement;

**(c)** the Client's use of the Services in violation of Applicable Law or the Acceptable Use Policy.

**17.3 Indemnification Procedures**

**(a)** The indemnified party shall:

- **(i)** promptly notify the indemnifying party in writing of any claim;
- **(ii)** cooperate with the indemnifying party in the defense of the claim;
- **(iii)** grant the indemnifying party sole control over the defense and settlement of the claim.

**(b)** The indemnifying party shall not settle any claim in a manner that admits liability on behalf of the indemnified party or imposes obligations on the indemnified party without the indemnified party's prior written consent.

**(c)** The indemnified party may participate in the defense at its own expense.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, EULA

## 8. EXPORT CONTROLS

### Standard Template (All Agreements)

**25.1 Compliance**

The Client shall comply with all applicable export control laws and regulations, including the U.S. Export Administration Regulations (EAR), the International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), and economic sanctions programs administered by the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC).

**25.2 Prohibited Territories**

The Client shall not access or use the Services from, or export Client Data to, any country or territory subject to comprehensive U.S. trade embargoes or sanctions, including Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and the Crimea region of Ukraine.

**25.3 Representations**

The Client represents and warrants that:

**(a)** it is not located in, under the control of, or a national or resident of any prohibited territory;

**(b)** it is not listed on any U.S. government list of prohibited or restricted parties, including the Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List;

**(c)** it will not permit Authorized Users who are located in or nationals of any prohibited territory to access the Services.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, EULA, B2C ToS

## 9. ANTI-CORRUPTION AND SANCTIONS

### Standard Template (All Agreements)

**26.1 Compliance**

The parties shall comply with all applicable anti-corruption and anti-bribery laws, including:

**(a)** the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA);

**(b)** the UK Bribery Act 2010;

**(c)** OFAC sanctions programs.

**26.2 Representations**

Each party represents and warrants that:

**(a)** it has not and will not, directly or indirectly, offer, pay, promise to pay, or authorize the payment of any money or anything of value to any government official or any other person for the purpose of obtaining or retaining business or securing any improper advantage;

**(b)** it is not subject to any sanctions administered by OFAC, the U.S. Department of State, the United Nations, the European Union, or any other applicable sanctions authority.

**26.3 Termination for Violation**

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party violates this Section.

**Used in:** MSA, SLA, EULA

## 10. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

### Standard Template (B2B)

**22.1 Governing Law**

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the jurisdiction specified in Schedule 1, without regard to its conflicts of law principles.

**22.2 Jurisdiction**

The parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts specified in Schedule 1 for the resolution of any disputes arising out of or related to this Agreement.

**22.3 Dispute Resolution**

**(a)** Prior to initiating any legal proceedings, the parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute through negotiation between senior executives of each party.

**(b)** If the dispute is not resolved within thirty (30) days of the initial request for negotiation, either party may pursue legal remedies.

**22.4 Injunctive Relief**

Notwithstanding Section 22.3, either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm.
